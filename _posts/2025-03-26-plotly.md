During my Ph.D I focused on analytical training more than writing about it. I assumed that writing would improve naturally with practice, that intuition would be enough. It wasn’t. Scientific writing took as much time as the analysis itself, yet I approached it without any deliberate training or strategy.

During the final chapter, my focus narrowed to the flaws in the analysis rather than the overall findings. One paragraph even began with “Control sample was excluded from the first sequencing run because of this and that…”. I read that sentence multiple times without recognizing any issue; I was not seeing the forest for the trees. Instead of presenting the results, I started with what went wrong and buried the actual findings under unnecessary details.

If I want to continue doing research, I need to communicate my work clearly. Being merely a good writer is not enough; I need to become an excellent one. Yesterday, I decided to approach writing more deliberately and seek guidance from writing-focused books. I also recognize that many researchers face similar challenges, so I decided to write weekly blog posts documenting my progress, with the hope that others may benefit from this process.
